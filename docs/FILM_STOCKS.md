# SpectraFilm Stock Catalog

SpectraFilm ships with 28 spectral film and paper profiles. This reference groups every
bundled stock by its role in the photographic chain — the film you expose, the slide you
project, the cine negative you shoot, and the print medium that renders the final image.

In SpectraFilm a *film* is paired with a *print medium* (an RGB paper for still negatives,
or a motion-picture print film for cine negatives) to produce the final look. Reversal
(slide) stocks are positives that need no print stage. Stocks marked **tungsten** are
balanced for ~3200 K artificial light rather than ~5500 K daylight.

ISO and balance are taken from manufacturer datasheets where available; era reflects the
introduction of the modern emulsion the profile models, not the original brand name. Where
a year is approximate it is marked with `~`.

## Motion-Picture Print Film

The projection-print stocks onto which a cine negative is printed for theatrical release.
They define the screen look — contrast, blacks, and the saturated "film print" palette.
Balanced for the tungsten-halogen + KG3 lamp of a printer/projector.

| Name | Manufacturer | Balance | Era | Character |
|------|--------------|---------|-----|-----------|
| Kodak Vision Color Print Film 2383 | Kodak | Tungsten (print) | ~2000 | Standard release print; rich blacks, neutral highlights. |
| Kodak Vision Premier Color Print Film 2393 | Kodak | Tungsten (print) | ~1998 | Premium print; deeper blacks, brighter highlights, more saturation. |

## RGB Print Paper

RA-4 color negative papers — the final medium for still color negatives. In SpectraFilm a
negative film is paired with one of these papers to render its final color and contrast.
These are papers, not films, so they carry no ISO speed.

| Name | Manufacturer | Type | Era | Character |
|------|--------------|------|-----|-----------|
| Kodak Professional Portra Endura | Kodak | RA-4 paper | ~2005 | Low contrast; natural flesh tones, moderate saturation. |
| Kodak Professional Supra Endura | Kodak | RA-4 paper | ~2003 | Moderate contrast; accurate skin, livelier color. |
| Kodak Professional Ultra Endura | Kodak | RA-4 paper | ~2003 | High contrast; blacker blacks, bold saturation. |
| Kodak Professional Endura Premier | Kodak | RA-4 paper | 2010 | Kodak's last RA-4 paper; tunable contrast, 100+ yr stability. |
| Kodak Ektacolor Edge | Kodak | RA-4 paper | ~1997 | Consumer/minilab paper; natural skin, rich everyday color. |
| Fujifilm Crystal Archive Type II | Fujifilm | RA-4 paper | ~2005 | Benchmark Fuji paper; rich gradation, vivid color, great stability. |

## Notes & Caveats

- **Push variants** (Portra 800 Push +1/+2) are not separate emulsions but the same Portra
  800 film rated and developed at a higher exposure index (EI 1600 / EI 3200).
- **Years** mark the introduction of the *modern emulsion* the profile models, not the
  original brand launch (e.g. the Portra line was rebranded in 2010; Gold and C200 trace
  to the late 1980s / early 1990s). Approximate years are flagged with `~`.
- **Verita 200D** is a recent (2026) motion-picture release; its character notes follow
  Kodak's announcement materials rather than long-term field experience.
- Print-film and paper "balance" denotes the printing/viewing illuminant they are designed
  around, not a camera-exposure balance.
